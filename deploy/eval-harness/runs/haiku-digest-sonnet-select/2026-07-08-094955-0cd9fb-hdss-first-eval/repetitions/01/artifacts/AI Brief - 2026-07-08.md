## 🚨 Safety & Research

### Model Collapse Risk Escalates as Meta Synthetic Ad Blitz Floods Web with AI Content (July 5, 2026)

<cite index="71-2">Model collapse is a degenerative process, formally described in a 2024 paper published in Nature by Oxford University researcher Ilia Shumailov and colleagues, in which AI models trained on data produced by earlier AI models progressively lose quality and diversity. The degradation is insidious because the early stage — when rare, minority, and creative outputs begin disappearing from the model's range — is invisible in standard benchmark scores. Only in late-stage collapse does overall performance visibly deteriorate.</cite>

<cite index="71-4">By one estimate cited in published research, 74 percent of newly created web pages contained AI-generated text as of early 2025. The numerators keep climbing. Meta's Brand Memory feature, and the broader Advantage+ system of which it is a part, contributes to that contamination indirectly. Every AI-generated ad that runs on Instagram and Facebook, every synthetic image variant produced by Meta's creative automation tools, flows back into the shared ecosystem.</cite>

<cite index="71-3">Collapse is avoidable when human data accumulates alongside synthetic data rather than being displaced by it. Aggressive dataset filtering, provenance tooling such as the Coalition for Content Provenance and Authenticity's C2PA standard, and content watermarking are all real mitigation approaches. The practical challenge is that these mitigations are active, ongoing costs — filtering pipelines to maintain, watermarks to embed, licensing deals to negotiate. Labs with Meta's financial scale can absorb those costs more easily than smaller or open-source teams.</cite>
